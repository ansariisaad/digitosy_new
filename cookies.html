Cookies Policy

This policy is effective as of 2th March 2022

A cookie is a small text file stored on your device when you visit websites. These cookies are used to remember your preferences, login details, and browsing activities. By storing this information, cookies help improve the website's functionality and your overall user experience.

For instance, they allow websites to remember your login status, language preferences, or the contents of your shopping cart. While cookies are generally safe, they can also collect sensitive information, which makes understanding their role crucial for online privacy.

2. What Types of Cookies Are There?

2.1. Essential cookies

These cookies are fundamental for the basic operation of the website, such as allowing you to log in securely or keep track of your session. Without them, essential services like authentication or form submissions would not work properly.

2.2. Performance cookies

Performance cookies collect anonymous information about how visitors use a website. They monitor data like page load times, user behavior, and errors encountered, helping improve the site’s functionality and speed.

2.3. Functionality cookies

Functionality cookies remember the choices you make, like language, region, or font size. These cookies allow a website to tailor its experience to better suit your needs, providing a more personalized and comfortable browsing experience.

2.4. Targeting/advertising cookies

These cookies are used to deliver ads that are relevant to your interests. They track your activity across different websites and gather data that helps advertisers show you personalized ads based on your browsing history.

3. Third-Party Cookies on Our Site

In addition to our own cookies, third-party cookies from advertising networks, analytics services, or social media platforms may be placed on your device when you visit our site. These third-party cookies collect data to analyze user behavior, show relevant ads, or track ad performance across various platforms. We partner with these services to help enhance your experience and provide you with more tailored content.

3.1. Targeting/advertising cookies

These third-party cookies are used to track your online behavior and serve you targeted ads on other websites. They gather information about your browsing history, preferences, and interactions to create a profile that advertisers use to display relevant ads. These cookies ensure you see more personalized advertisements rather than generic ones.

4. Cookies and Your Personal Information

Cookies can sometimes collect personal information like your IP address, browsing preferences, and login details. While cookies help enhance your user experience, they can also raise privacy concerns. We use cookies responsibly and in compliance with data protection laws to ensure that your personal information remains safe and confidential.

Rest assured, any information collected via cookies is used to improve our services and is never shared without your consent.

5. How to Configure and Disable Cookies

Most browsers give you control over how cookies are stored and managed.

  • You can easily configure or disable cookies through your browser’s privacy settings. This allows you to block all cookies, limit certain types, or receive notifications when cookies are being placed on your device.

  • However, disabling essential cookies may prevent certain website features from working correctly, affecting your browsing experience.
